What is Kidney Disease?

Kidney Disease: Kidney disease can be acute or chronic. Acute kidney disease often occurs suddenly and may be reversible with prompt treatment. Chronic kidney disease (CKD) is a long-term condition that progresses over time, and in severe cases, it can lead to kidney failure, also known as end-stage renal disease (ESRD).

Symptoms
  • Changes in Urination
  • Swelling
  • Blood in Urine
  • Fatigue
  • High Blood Pressure

Side Effects

  • Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)
  • End-Stage Renal Disease (ESRD)
  • Cardiovascular Complications
  • Anemia
  • Uremia
  • Fluid and Electrolyte Imbalances

Preventing kidney disease involves managing risk factors such as diabetes and hypertension, maintaining a healthy lifestyle, and staying hydrated. For individuals with kidney disease, management includes medication, dietary changes, and, in advanced cases, renal replacement therapy (dialysis or transplantation).
