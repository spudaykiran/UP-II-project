What is Heart Disease?

Heart Disease: Heart disease occurs when the blood vessels that supply blood to the heart muscle become narrowed or blocked due to the buildup of cholesterol and other substances, restricting blood flow. This reduced blood flow can lead to chest pain (angina) or a heart attack if the blood supply is completely blocked.

Symptoms
  • Chest pain or Discomfort
  • Shortness of breath
  • Rapid or irregular Heartbeat
  • Fatigue
  • Dizziness or Fainting

Side Effects

  • Heart Attack (Myocardial Infarction)
  • Heart Failure
  • Arrhythmias
  • Cardiomyopathy
  • Peripheral Artery Disease (PAD)
  • Valve Problems

Preventive measures and management of heart disease often involve lifestyle changes such as a healthy diet, regular exercise, not smoking, and managing conditions like diabetes and hypertension. Medications and medical procedures may also be recommended based on the specific type and severity of heart disease.
