What is Diabetes?

Diabetes is a chronic medical condition that occurs when the body is unable to properly regulate blood sugar (glucose) levels.

There are two main types of diabetes:

Type 1 Diabetes: Typically diagnosed in children and young adults, caused by the immune system attacking insulin-producing cells.

Type 2 Diabetes: Common in adults, characterized by insulin resistance and inadequate insulin production.

Symptoms
  • Frequent urination
  • Increased hunger
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fatigue
  • Blurred vision
  • Slow wound healing

Side Effects

  • Cardiovascular problems
  • Kidney damage
  • Eye problems
  • Nerve damage
  • Foot problems
  • Skin conditions

Managing diabetes involves maintaining blood sugar levels within a target range through a combination of medication, lifestyle modifications, and regular monitoring. Regular medical check-ups are crucial to detect and address any potential complications early on.
